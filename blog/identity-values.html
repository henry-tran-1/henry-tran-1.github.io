Identity, Values and Strengths

DOPE personality test

Dove

Strengths:      People-orientated, loyal, friendly, hardworking and a great team player.
Limitations:  Tends to avoid change, confrontation, risk-taking and assertiveness.

Values and Identity

My family came to New Zealand as refugees and although I was very young, I still remember the difficulties and struggles my parents went through to provide a life for my brother and I. My parents instilled in me the importance of hardwork and determination.

Working in primary healthcare as an optometrist has taught me the value of patience, kindness and humility. These are now values I hold important to my identity.

Strengths

I believe the DOPE personality test was quite accurate, and I would agree my strengths are:

  • Hardworking
  • Team player
  • Friendly

I work well in a team environment, where I'm happy to learn from others and to teach others what I've learned. These traits should help me do well at DA bootcamp.

Limitations

I do tend to avoid confrontation and prefer to keep the peace. This may hinder my learning and development if I choose not to speak up if I'm having problems.
I also recognise that I can sometimes lose confidence if I go through long periods of struggle.

Ethics and Conflict resolution

When I worked in a corporate optometry practice, one area of contention was management's policy of pushing glasses sales. They would encourage us to recommend glasses even in cases where the patient may not have benefited.
I chose in the end not to adhere to these policies, and would sometimes fall short of my KPIs and bonuses. I remember at the time thinking that the reward of meeting expectations and monetary bonuses were not worth compromising my own values of sincerity and fairness. I look back favourably on most of my decisions in the clinic.

I would sometimes help Dad with projects he has around the house, but one of his big flaws (in my opinion) is his inability to take on constructive feedback.
Giving suggestions when I recognised problems/inefficient solutions were never received well, and eventually I would stop trying altogether.
Looking back now, I recognise my way of expressing these suggestions were probably blunt and not conducive to being heard as constructive feedback.
I would now approach these situations with more focus on empathy and listening to the other party.
