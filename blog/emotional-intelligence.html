Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

It is the ability to monitor and control your own emotions and feelings, as well as reading another person's emotions and be able to handle relationships with others in a fair and empathetic way.

How is EQ different to IQ?

Emotional quotient (EQ) refers to your emotional intelligence and how you can use your awareness of your own and other's emotions to guide your behaviour. These are better known as your social skills, such as: regulating your emotions; empathy for others; and communicating effectively.
Intelligence quotient (IQ) refers to your intellectual ability, such as your capacity to learn, comprehend and apply knowledge.
They are both important skills: EQ helps you navigate social and emotional situations while IQ helps you use your cognitive ability to understand and overcome challenges.

What is EQ important?

Having a strong EQ is important because it comes with many benefits: better at communication; better at using your social awareness in problem solving; and better at regulating your emotions which allows you to perform at a higher level. All these benefits will make someone a better learner and a better candidate for hire.
