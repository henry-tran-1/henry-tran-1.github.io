Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people
By knowing that your intelligence is malleable and that you can physically alter your brain and its connections, you can improve your ability to learn and perform better at tasks you might previously have thought impossible. The results of a study (Child Development, 2007) showed that students did significant better than their peers in a control group, when they understood this concept.

How you might use this for your own benefit
Understanding neuroplasticity means understanding I can improve my brain function and its ability to learn. I consequently know that I am not limited by what I perceive to be the ceiling of my capabilities.
When I encounter difficulties in the DA course or problems that seem impossible, I know that eventually I'll be able to handle them and think of solutions that weren't obvious to me at the beginning.

Ways to increase neuroplasticity
One way is to practice and repeat a certain activity many times. This stimulates your brain (specifically the neural pathway that is responsible for this activity) and will over time, strengthen its connections. This results in a stronger and more efficient pathway that is capable of better performance.
Another way is to tap into already-existing pathways. This can be done by integrating that activity with another subject that you are already familar with (thus already having a strengthened neural pathway). Or by thinking of difficult concepts in analogies that you are familiar with and are easier to understand (and again already having an already-existing neural pathway).

Growth Mindset

What is it, and why is it relevant
A growth mindset is when you can react positively/constructively after encountering an obstacle or a tough challenge: you understand that you aren't able to overcome this challenge at the moment, but you can learn to eventually. A fixed mindset is when you react in a negative manner: you do not believe you can improve your ability/intelligence to do so at any point and therefore give up.
This is relevant because we will likely encounter many difficult concepts and challenges in this DA course and we should react to them by improving ourselves as opposed to giving up.

What changed for me after exploring this topic
By knowing there are studies and evidence that support this idea, it definitely makes me more determined to go through life with a growth mindset.

How to use this concept in my own learning journey
I will treat obstacles and challenges as opportunities where I can further develop my programming ability. With the help of the course and facilitators, I am confident and believe that I will eventually overcome all these future roadblocks.

Changes to my learning plan and strategy

By giving myself this time, I will be able to practice and improve the relevant neural pathways. And knowing that my ability to learn is malleable, this strategy will give myself a better chance to grow and develop myself.
